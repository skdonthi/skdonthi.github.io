About

Customer-first product engineer
who learned to ship AI.

A decade in product engineering taught me that great software starts with the people using it, not the framework you pick. I bring that same discipline to AI: understand the operator, the domain, and the workflow first; choose the model and the stack second.

At Xcontrol I sit aboard vessels with customers before I write a line of code. I run discovery sessions with cruise line operators and ship IT crews, capture what actually breaks in daily operations, and translate that into product specs the team can build against, then into AI features that fit the workflow rather than the demo reel.

I hold a high bar for what production means: observable, resilient, explainable, and fast enough that no one reaches for the spreadsheet instead. I'm newer to applied AI than I am to product engineering, and I treat that as an advantage. I ship AI the way I've shipped products for ten years: small, useful, and accountable to the customer.

How I Work

Principles I operate by.

01

Be Obsessed with Your Customer

I run discovery sessions with operators before writing a line of code. Real problems, not assumed ones, drive every architecture decision.

02

Raise the Bar

Flakiness is a blocker, not an edge case. I hold production to a standard where observable, resilient, and fast are non-negotiable minimums.

03

Better Together

The best outcomes come from engineering, operations, and customers in the same conversation. I work to close that gap, not widen it.

04

Act as an Owner

I take end-to-end responsibility: from requirements through deployment, onboarding ship IT teams, and staying available when things break at sea.

05

Find Joy in What You Do

Whether debugging an agent loop or explaining RAG to a crew member who's never heard of an LLM, I find the work genuinely worth doing.

06

Your Principle
